About

The Strand Bookstore, located in New York City, was founded in 1927 by Benjamin Bass. It originally occupied a small space on Fourth Avenue's famous "Book Row," a stretch of six city blocks that housed over 40 bookstores. The Strand outlasted its neighbors to become a literary institution, renowned for its extensive collection of new, used, and rare books. Over the decades, the bookstore expanded, moving to its current location on Broadway and 12th Street in 1957. It boasts over 18 miles of bookshelves and remains family-owned, now operated by the third generation of the Bass family. The Strand is celebrated for its eclectic inventory, author events, and its role as a cultural landmark in New York City's vibrant literary scene.

The Strand Bookstore has become synonymous with New York City's literary culture, attracting book lovers from around the world. Its iconic red awning and slogan, "18 Miles of Books," are instantly recognizable, symbolizing the store's vast and diverse collection. The Strand offers everything from bestsellers and academic texts to rare first editions and out-of-print volumes. It also features a bustling Rare Book Room, where bibliophiles can find valuable and unique treasures. Beyond books, the store sells literary-themed merchandise and hosts numerous author readings, signings, and book clubs, fostering a vibrant community of readers and writers. Despite the challenges faced by independent bookstores in the digital age, The Strand continues to thrive, maintaining its status as a beloved and enduring fixture in the city's cultural landscape.
